Who was Stan Lee?

Stan Lee was an American comic book writer, editor, publisher, and producer. He co-created numerous popular fictional characters, including superheroes like Spider-Man, the X-Men, Iron Man, Thor, Hulk, the Fantastic Four, Black Panther, Daredevil, Doctor Strange, Scarlet Witch and Ant-Man.

  • Born : as Stanley Martin Lieber on December 28, 1922 in New York, U.S.
  • Died : November 12, 2018 (aged 95) in Los Angeles, California, U.S.
  • Spouse : Joan Boocock (m. 1947; died 2017)
  • Awards : The Will Eisner Award Hall of Fame, Jack Kirby Hall of Fame, National Medal of Arts, Disney Legends

    • Early Life and Career

    Stan Lee was born to Romanian immigrants Celia and Jack Lieber and had a younger brother named Larry. His childhood was spent under the Great Depression with his family stuggling to make both ends meet.

    He was hired as an office assistant at Timely Comics in 1939 and became an interim editor for the company in the early 1940s. He also served domestically in the Army during World War II, working as a writer and illustrator.

    The First Big Break

    In the early '60s, he co-created a series with Jack Kirby for Marvel Comics (Timely's new name) to compete with rival DC Comics and the Fantastic Four was born in 1961.Following the success of the Fantastic Four,he along with his other Marvel creators created characters like the Hulk, Spider-Man, Doctor Strange, Daredevil and the X-Men.

    Marvel Comics became a highly popular franchise, and Stan Lee was promoted to editorial director and publisher in 1972.

    Motto

    "Excelsior" means 'upward and onward to greater glory'.

    Death

    Lee died at the age of 95 at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles, California, on November 12, 2018 due to respiratory and congestive heart failure.
